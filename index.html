Sells Out to StubSham

  • Oh look it's a sticker
• 2020
6.9
  • by Chicken Hernandez
    Bird of Prey Expert
  • Drugs/Tickets
  • Electronic
19 hrs ago

Five years after doing tons of acid, Teh Raptor has finally found a way to sellout an entire community; intimate and unvarnished, we all knew this was coming, it was merely a question of: When? Discussion was rampant. "There are just so many tickets in there" said a person, "now we can't even buy tickets because of this Coronavirus bullshit, what are we supposed to do?" said another person. Well the solution became quite clear when the Stub connection presented itself; sell tickets for imaginary shows.

Now live: StubSham

Featured Tracks:

I don't recommend — Pressing playVia
Divine Intervention / Buy

Frankfurt Reynolds called Teh on a slightly warm March afternoon. As the COO (Chief Ovis Officer) of StubHub he saw a major opportunity. StubHub and the events world reeling from banned gatherings interacted with a raptor community itching to get the rush of purchasing tickets, and the sweet sweet service fees that come with them. "It all made too much sense" said a renowned philosopher and music expert, with about 80% of all people agreeing. "You just can't argue with those numbers" said Frankfurt, "when you see numbers like that, you just go for it, and we didn't really have a choice since our entire business model is based upon being the top tier criminals of the events industry."

But the solution did not come to Frankfurt immediately. "I think we had our slaves go on about ten or so coffee runs. You should've seen the boardroom, we were all going beserk trying to figure out the right solution, or the ethical thing to do, until we realized we are only good at being evil. We merely had to up the ante in order to bring the stock back up." So with StubHub finally accepting the fact that they are a truly evil force in the music world, the question became: what is the next level of socioenomic manipulation that can be induced unto all of those strange people who love music, but have no shows to attend?

"Well anyway, I started blasting ideas. Community that's a word I hear a lot, but what does it actually mean? Is it even a word? Does it even have a meaning?" Community is in fact a word with meaning, and there has been about a factor of 8 increase of people in executive positions (independent of race or gender) who have now taken the time to look it up on Wikipedia- some even sort of understand it. "I had heard about this raptor community, and that sounded hip. Then when someone told me that they seamlessly giveaway and sell tickets under facevalue, I practically spat my coffee out! Imagine the transactions!" People, tickets, hip, it was the perfect plan. Utilize a community that already enjoys the act of buying and selling tickets, ignore the fact that the important tenets are actually based on real connection, and you save StubHub. God bless America.

That's when Teh received the call. She did not care about most of what Frank was saying, in fact she was at least three degrees of freedom too high to understand 3 out of 4 english words. "While trying to figure out where to put my feet, I could barely discern that I did in fact have another strange entitled white man on the line trying to pitch yet another shallow idea to monetize the Concert Raptor community. I had been keeping a log of such encounters, and for whatever reason a strange percentage of them have the names [REDACTED] or [REDACTED] (mysteriously never seen in the same room). Three years ago I told myself if for some reason I did not find a gem mine while wandering aimlessly through New Mexico, I would sell the raptor community to the thirteenth asshole to pitch an idea to me who wasn't named [REDACTED] or [REDACTED]. Now that I was back in LA petting my black labs, the gem mine dream was officially over. The call, his name being Frankfurt, it was wonderful serendipity!"

The idea is quite simple. Give the raptors a platform to spend all of their money, and the mere idea of attending a show, plus the added benefit of FOMO and exclusivity, and you are printing money from other people's effort once again. And what happens when the pandemic ends? Well Teh will just put a blockade on all tickets that do not go through the platform. Sevice fees galore. But what about exploiting a good natured community? That is just the cherry on top, as StubHub wouldn't have it any other way. "Someone has to keep the blood running through our underground river", Frank declared while staring at a mountain, "those vampires don't feed themselves, they haven't for thousands of years, and hell if I'm willing to start caring about humanity."
